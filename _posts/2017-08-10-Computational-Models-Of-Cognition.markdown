Preliminary Thoughts
---------------------

#### **Is brain the only place where the cognition happens?**

It is very true that our brain plays a very important role in cognition. However, there
is the theory of [Embodied cognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embodied_cognition) which indicates that the entire body of an organism shapes the cognitive abilities. 

Say we had a very powerful computing machine and could simulate the neuron connections in the human brain, 
Can we generate natural-intelligence ? There is a gap in our understanding of how the brain works, but if
we truely understand all the underlying physiology of human brain and emulate it on a machine is that sufficient 
for intelligence is a hard question to answer. Philosophically speaking it is possible there some meta-physical element is attached to human intelligence which we haven't discovered. However, it is important to see what we can 
engineer than debate about philosophical aspects. 

Also, there is question of Build vs Grow in Aritifical Intelligence. Human intelligence is shaped by experiences and memory, this enhances over time this is the "Growth of Intelligence". However, many present day models for the task of language translation is a "Built Intelligence" we show it a lot of adult conversations/language during training, So the model has not learnt say the translation ability like how a human does.
